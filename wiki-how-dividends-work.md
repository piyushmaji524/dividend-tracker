**Example:**

- You own: 100 shares before
- You own: 140 shares after (100 + 40 bonus)

## ğŸ”‘ The Three Critical Dates

### 1. Ex-Dividend Date âš ï¸
**Most Important!**

**What it means:** Last day to buy and receive dividend

**How it works:**
```
TIMELINE:
Before Ex-Date: Buy here â†’ GET dividend âœ“
Ex-Date: Special date, stock opens lower
After Ex-Date: Buy here â†’ DON'T get dividend âœ—
```

**Why stock drops on ex-date:**
- Dividend is being paid out
- Company has less cash
- New buyers don't get upcoming dividend
- Price adjusts down by ~dividend amount

**Example:**
- Stock price before ex-date: â‚¹1,000
- Dividend announced: â‚¹50
- Stock price on ex-date: â‚¹950 (approx)
- Drop = Dividend amount

---

### 2. Record Date ğŸ“

**What it means:** Date company "records" shareholders

**How it works:**
1. Ex-dividend date passes
2. Company takes a "snapshot"
3. Records all shareholders as of this date
4. These recorded shareholders get dividend

**Why it matters:**
- Establishes who gets dividend
- Usually day after ex-dividend date
- Must own shares BEFORE this date
- Since most trades settle in T+1, buying before ex-date ensures record date ownership

---

### 3. Distribution Date ğŸ’³

**What it means:** Date dividend is actually credited

**How it works:**
1. Company processes all dividend payments
2. Transfers money to shareholders' accounts
3. Money appears in your bank/brokerage

**Timeline:**
```
Record Date: Nov 23 (recorded as shareholder)
Processing: Nov 24 - Nov 28 (company processes)
Distribution Date: Nov 29 (money in your account!)
```

**Typical gap:** 5-7 days from record date

### Formula

### Example 1: TCS

## ğŸ”„ Dividend Frequency

### Annual Dividends
**Frequency:** Once per year
**Companies:** Many Indian companies
**Example:** Full year dividend announced in Jan-Mar

### Quarterly Dividends
**Frequency:** 4 times per year
**Companies:** Some larger companies
**Schedule:** Usually Jan, Apr, Jul, Oct
**Benefit:** Regular income stream

### Special Dividends
**Frequency:** Occasional
**When:** Company has excess cash
**Amount:** One-time, usually higher
**Benefit:** Bonus for shareholders

### Dividend Policy by Company
- **Conservative:** Annual dividend only
- **Shareholder-friendly:** Quarterly dividends
- **Aggressive:** Special + quarterly + annual

## ğŸ’¼ Dividend Payout Ratio

### What It Means
Percentage of earnings paid out as dividends.

### Formula
```
Payout Ratio % = (Dividend per Share / Earnings per Share) Ã— 100
```

### Examples

**Low Payout (20-30%):**
- Company keeps most profits for growth
- Lower dividend now, potential higher growth
- Growth companies

**Moderate Payout (40-60%):**
- Balance between dividend and growth
- Stable, mature companies
- Ideal for dividend investors

**High Payout (70-100%):**
- Most profit goes to shareholders
- Little for growth
- Mature, declining companies
- Risk: Unsustainable if earnings fall

### What to Look For
```
âœ“ 40-60%: Ideal for dividend stocks
âœ“ Consistent: Company maintains payout
âœ— >100%: Paying more than earning (risky!)
âœ— Declining: Payout ratio going up (earnings falling)
```
