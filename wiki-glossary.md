### Dividend
**Definition:** A payment made by a corporation to its shareholders, usually in cash or additional shares.

### Ex-Dividend Date
**Definition:** The last day you can buy a stock and receive the upcoming dividend.

**How it works:**
- Buy BEFORE ex-date ‚Üí You get dividend ‚úì
- Buy ON or AFTER ex-date ‚Üí You don't get dividend ‚úó

**Example Timeline:**
```

Dec 18: Last day to buy (buy today, get dividend)
Dec 20: First day not eligible (too late)
```

**Why it matters:** This is the KEY date for dividend eligibility!

**Related:** Record Date, Distribution Date

---

### Record Date
**Definition:** The date the company officially records shareholders in their database.

**How it works:**
- Company takes a "snapshot" of shareholders
- You must own shares on this date to be recorded
- Company sends dividends to recorded shareholders

**Timeline Relationship:**
```
Ex-Dividend Date: Dec 19
Record Date: Dec 20 (next day usually)
Distribution Date: Dec 26
```

**Important:** You should buy BEFORE ex-dividend date to be on record date.

**Related:** Ex-Dividend Date, Distribution Date

---

### Distribution Date
**Definition:** The date when the dividend is actually credited to your bank account or brokerage.

**How it works:**
1. Ex-Dividend Date passes
2. Record Date passes
3. Company processes payments
4. Distribution Date: Money arrives in your account

**Timeline:**
```
Dec 19: Ex-Dividend Date
Dec 20: Record Date
Dec 26: Distribution Date (you get money!)
```

**Typical Gap:** Usually 6-7 days after record date

**Related:** Ex-Dividend Date, Record Date

### PE Ratio (Price-to-Earnings)
**Definition:** Stock price divided by annual earnings per share. Measures if a stock is expensive or cheap.

**Formula:** Stock Price / Earnings per Share (EPS)

**Example:**
- Stock price: ‚Çπ100
- Earnings per share: ‚Çπ10

**Interpretation:**
- **Low PE (10-15):** Stock is cheap relative to earnings
- **High PE (>25):** Stock is expensive
- **Average PE (15-25):** Fairly valued

**Comparison:** Compare within same sector

**Related:** Valuation, Stock Price, Earnings

### 52-Week High & Low
**Definition:** The highest and lowest stock prices in the past 52 weeks (1 year).

**What it means:**
- **52-Week High:** Maximum price stock reached
- **52-Week Low:** Minimum price stock reached
- **Range:** Shows stock volatility

**Example:**
- 52-Week High: ‚Çπ1,200
- Current Price: ‚Çπ1,000
- 52-Week Low: ‚Çπ800
- Stock trading near middle of range (relatively stable)

**Interpretation:**
- Trading near high = strong momentum
- Trading near low = potential upside
- Wide range = more volatile

**Related:** Volatility, Price Range, Support/Resistance

### Share / Stock
**Definition:** A unit of ownership in a company.

**How it works:**
- Company divides ownership into shares
- You buy shares to become partial owner
- More shares = higher ownership %

**Example:**
- Company has 100 shares
- You buy 10 shares
- You own 10% of company

**Rights of shareholders:**
- Receive dividends
- Vote in company decisions
- Claim on company assets

**Related:** Ownership, Equity

### Risk
**Definition:** Possibility of losing money on investment.

**Types of Risk:**
- **Company Risk:** One company underperforms
- **Sector Risk:** Entire sector downturn
- **Market Risk:** Entire stock market falls
- **Interest Rate Risk:** Rates rise, bond prices fall

**Risk Reduction:**
- Diversification
- Long-term holding
- Dividend focus
- Quality companies

**Related:** Volatility, Return, Reward

### Liquidity
**Definition:** How easily you can buy/sell an investment.

**High Liquidity:**
- Easy to buy & sell
- Many buyers & sellers
- Quick transactions
- Most NSE stocks have good liquidity

**Low Liquidity:**
- Hard to sell
- Few buyers & sellers
- Slow transactions
- Small cap stocks may have lower liquidity

**Related:** Trading Volume, Bid-Ask Spread

**Dividend focus:** Perfect for long-term investing

## üí° Strategy Terms

### Dividend Investing
**Definition:** Strategy of buying stocks primarily for their dividends.

**Approach:**
1. Find quality dividend-paying stocks
2. Buy and hold
3. Receive regular dividends
4. Reinvest dividends for compounding

**Benefits:**
- Regular income stream
- Lower volatility
- Compounding growth
- Less active management

**Ideal for:** Retirement, passive income

**Related:** Income Investing, Passive Income

### Dividend Growth
**Definition:** Strategy of investing in stocks with growing dividends over time.

**How it works:**
- Company increases dividend each year
- You benefit from higher payments
- Also stock price may appreciate

**Example:**
- Year 1: ‚Çπ10 dividend
- Year 2: ‚Çπ12 dividend (+20%)

### Value Investing
**Definition:** Buying stocks that are underpriced relative to their value.

**How to identify:**
- Low PE ratio
- High dividend yield
- Strong fundamentals
- Trading below intrinsic value

**Goal:** Buy low, sell high (eventually)

**Famous Investors:** Warren Buffett uses this strategy

**Related:** PE Ratio, Undervalued, Fundamental Analysis
